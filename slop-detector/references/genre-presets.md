# Genre Presets

Each genre has different tolerances and red flags. Load the matching preset before scoring.

---

## LinkedIn Posts

**Priority**: personality > naturalness > brevity > clarity

**Extra-sensitive to**:
- "Thrilled to announce" / "Humbled to share" / "Excited to" (instant high-severity)
- Numbered takeaway lists ("3 things I learned from...")
- Ending with a generic question: "What do you think?" / "Agree?"
- Hashtag spam (3+ hashtags at the end)
- "I" in every sentence without self-awareness about it
- Humble-brag structures: problem → I solved it → audience praise
- Corporate-inspirational tone blending personal anecdote with business advice

**More tolerant of**:
- Casual register, sentence fragments, informal punctuation
- Short paragraphs (even one-sentence paragraphs — that's a LinkedIn norm)
- Direct address to the reader
- Personal anecdotes, even slightly embellished

**Structural red flags**:
- Hook line designed to make you click "see more" → 3 points → CTA
- Every paragraph is exactly one sentence (over-optimized for mobile readability)
- Empty line between every sentence

**Target voice**: Sounds like the person talking to a colleague over coffee, not presenting at a conference. Specific. Occasionally blunt. Doesn't perform gratitude.

**Score adjustments**: +5 for "thrilled/honored/humbled" opening. +5 for numbered lessons format. +3 for "Agree?" or "Thoughts?" closing.

## Cover Letters

**Priority**: personality > clarity > professional tone > naturalness

**Extra-sensitive to**:
- "I am writing to express my interest in" (the single most AI-detectable opening in existence)
- "I am a highly motivated [noun] with a proven track record"
- "I am confident that my skills and experience make me an ideal candidate"
- "I would welcome the opportunity to discuss"
- Mirroring job posting language word-for-word without adding anything
- Generic company praise: "I admire [company]'s commitment to innovation"
- Every paragraph starting with "I"

**More tolerant of**:
- Slightly formal register (this is appropriate for the genre)
- Structured format (intro, body, close) — this is expected
- Mentioning specific qualifications and experiences

**Structural red flags**:
- Three paragraphs of exactly equal length
- Every claim is qualified ("I believe I could contribute")
- Closing paragraph that is pure filler with no specific ask

**Target voice**: Direct and specific. Shows you researched the company by referencing something concrete, not by saying "your innovative culture." Opens with why you care, not with a formal announcement that you're applying. Closes with what you'd do in the first 90 days, not with a generic "looking forward to hearing from you."

**Score adjustments**: +10 for "I am writing to express my interest." +5 for "proven track record." +5 for generic company praise without specifics.

## Recruiter Messages

**Priority**: naturalness > brevity > personality > clarity

**Extra-sensitive to**:
- "I came across your profile and was impressed by"
- "I have an exciting opportunity"
- "I think you'd be a great fit for"
- "Are you open to exploring new opportunities?"
- Long messages (recruiter messages should be 3-5 sentences max)
- Listing company perks before saying what the job actually is
- Flattery without specificity: "your impressive background"

**More tolerant of**:
- Direct, transactional tone
- Brief, even abrupt
- Skip pleasantries

**Target voice**: Get to the point in under 60 words. Say the company name, the role, the one thing that makes it interesting for THIS person specifically. Then ask if they want to talk. That's it.

**Score adjustments**: +10 for "exciting opportunity" without saying what it is. +5 for any message over 150 words. +5 for "I was impressed by your background" without citing anything specific.

---

## General / Everything Else

**Priority**: personality > naturalness > brevity > clarity > professional tone

**Default heuristic weights apply.** No genre-specific bonuses or tolerances.

**Includes**: blog posts, articles, emails, reports, social media (non-LinkedIn), bios, about pages, product copy, documentation prose.

**Note**: If the text fits a more specific genre above, use that preset instead. "General" is the fallback, not the default.
