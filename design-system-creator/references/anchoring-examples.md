# Anchoring Examples — Specificity Calibration

This document shows the thought process for arriving at an anchoring. It serves as a reference to calibrate the expected level of specificity. A good anchoring CANNOT be reused for another project in the same sector.

---

## Structure of an anchoring

```
SUBJECT → PHYSICAL/SENSORY WORLD → DOMINANT MATERIAL → ANCHORING
```

The anchoring is a sentence that begins with: **"The design system is anchored in..."**

---

## Full examples

### 1. Zinc artisan (furniture and decoration in zinc, copper, brass)

**Sensory exploration:**
- What do I see? → Matte and glossy metallic surfaces, orange-toned copper reflections, raw blue-grey zinc, golden brass gleam. Workshop lighting — cold fluorescents + warm metal.
- What do I touch? → The cold of metal, the roughness of the weld, the smoothness of polished metal, the grain of the patina.
- What changes with time? → The patina. Zinc turns green, copper oxidizes, brass tarnishes. Time is what gives value.

**Anchoring:** "The design system is anchored in the metals themselves — the blue-grey of zinc that patinas with time, the warm gleam of copper that brings life, the rare gold of brass that distinguishes."

**What it produces:**
- Foundation: warm charcoal (workshop), not cold black (tech)
- Action: copper — the only warm color, reserved for interactive elements
- Distinction: brass — rare, reserved for badges and premium labels
- Typography: serif with fine serifs (precision of the craft) + discreet sans-serif (the metal speaks, not the text)
- Radius: 2-3px max (metal has edges, not bubbles)

**Specificity test:** if I replace "zinc artisan" with "ironwork artisan", it no longer holds — ironwork is about black forged iron, scrollwork, orange forge glow. Completely different palette. ✅

### 2. Law firm specializing in business law

**Sensory exploration:**
- What do I see? → Dark wood bookshelves, leather bindings, thick vellum paper, black ink, brass desk lamps.
- What do I touch? → The grain of laid paper, the smooth leather of armchairs, the waxed wood of meeting tables.
- What endures? → Legal precedent. A ruling from 1873 carries as much weight as one from 2024. Durability is the core of the profession.

**Anchoring:** "The design system is anchored in the study — laid paper, the black ink of legal texts, the leather of bindings, the waxed wood of bookshelves where time accumulates instead of eroding."

**What it produces:**
- Foundation: laid cream (not pure white — the paper has grain), near-black text at warm temperature
- Action: ink — a very dark blue, almost black, that lightens on hover like fresh ink
- Distinction: brass/dark gold — the desk lamp, the monogram
- Typography: classic serif (Caslon, Garamond) for the legal tradition + humanist sans-serif for the body
- Radius: 0px. Right angles. The law is rectangular.

**Specificity test:** a law firm specializing in digital law → the anchoring does not hold. This one is about paper, leather, timelessness. Digital would call for an anchoring around the screen, code, signal. ✅

### 3. Guided meditation app

**Sensory exploration:**
- What do I perceive with eyes closed? → Morning fog, the diffuse light of dawn, the smooth stone of a zen garden, the sound of flowing water.
- What is NOT there? → Strong contrast, vivid colors, visual noise. Absence is a presence.
- What is the movement? → Slow, continuous, cyclical. The breath.

**Anchoring:** "The design system is anchored in dawn — the moment when light is diffuse, outlines are blurred, colors are desaturated, and silence is still a sound."

**What it produces:**
- Foundation: off-white lavender (mist), stone grey text (not black — too harsh)
- Action: a very soft dawn blue, almost grey — just enough color to guide
- No distinction family — visual scarcity IS the design
- Typography: rounded thin sans-serif (like Figtree or Nunito) for everything — no serif, no contrast, the text never shouts
- Radius: pill or very rounded — no edges, no angles
- Animations: transition-slow by default. Nothing jumps.

**Specificity test:** a sleep app → the anchoring would be twilight/night, not dawn. Dark palette, not light. ✅

### 4. E-commerce analytics SaaS

**Sensory exploration:**
- What am I looking at? → A screen in the dark, luminous graphs, moving numbers. The dashboard at 11 PM when the founder checks conversions.
- What is the signal-to-noise ratio? → The signal is rare and precious. Noise is everywhere. The dashboard's job is to separate the two.
- What is the tension? → Every data point is a decision. Red is a loss, green is a gain. The emotion is in the numbers.

**Anchoring:** "The design system is anchored in the screen-in-the-dark — the dark background of the nocturnal dashboard, the luminous signal of the data that matters, the signal/noise contrast as a visual organization principle."

**What it produces:**
- Foundation: deep black (#0D0D0F) + very slightly lighter surfaces (#18181B)
- Action: a signal green (#22C55E family) — conversion, success, the number you want to see climb
- Structure: cold blue-grey (#64748B) — navigation, labels, everything that is not data
- Distinction: signal red (#EF4444) for alerts — but NEVER decorative, only semantic
- Typography: monospace or tabular-nums for data, dense sans-serif for body
- Radius: 6-8px — soft but not pill (the dashboard is a tool, not a toy)

**Specificity test:** a project management SaaS → the anchoring would be the kanban, the sticky note, the movement of cards. Not the screen-in-the-dark. ✅

### 5. Japanese omakase restaurant

**Sensory exploration:**
- What do I see? → The light hinoki wood counter, the irregular ceramic of the plates, the chef's knife, the fish on ice. A space where every object has its exact place.
- What do I NOT see? → The superfluous. Ma (間) — empty space — is as important as what fills it.
- What is the temperature? → Cold and precise (the fish, the knife, the ice) within a warm setting (the wood, the dim lighting).

**Anchoring:** "The design system is anchored in the omakase counter — the light hinoki wood, the imperfect ceramic, the void (ma) that gives value to each placed element, and the cold/warm tension of fish on wood."

**What it produces:**
- Foundation: light wood (#F4EDE4) as background, warm near-black text (#1A1814)
- Action: a discreet blood red (wasabi/umami) — only for reservations and CTAs
- No structure or distinction family — the void is the structure
- Typography: a thin, tall sans-serif for titles (Tenor Sans, Cormorant sans serif) + minimal body
- Radius: 0px or 2px max — ceramic and wood, not plastic
- Spacing: very generous. The ma. Every section breathes.

**Specificity test:** an Italian trattoria restaurant → the wood would be rustic, not hinoki. Ceramic would be absent, replaced by colorful faience. The void would be replaced by abundance. ✅

### 6. High-end titanium bike brand

**Sensory exploration:**
- What do I perceive? → Brushed titanium — grey with a slightly golden reflection. Wet asphalt. The wind. The clean line of the frame.
- What is the gesture? → Controlled speed. Not brutality — fluidity. Titanium is the metal that absorbs vibrations.
- What distinguishes titanium? → It does not rust, it does not scratch easily, it ages without changing. Permanence.

**Anchoring:** "The design system is anchored in brushed titanium — the grey with warm reflection that does not change, the taut line of the frame, asphalt as backdrop, and speed as silence."

**What it produces:**
- Foundation: asphalt grey (#2C2C2E) + light titanium surface (#E8E6E1)
- Action: a road orange (#E8673C) — road markings, signage, visibility
- Structure: brushed titanium (#9A9590) — navigation, borders, metallic elements
- Typography: condensed tall display sans-serif (Anybody, Bebas Neue) + technical body (Outfit)
- Radius: 0px — the frame is a tube, but the site is a line
- Images: full screen, no radius, strong contrast

**Specificity test:** a steel bike brand → the anchoring would be about potential rust, weight, tradition. Warmer, rougher palette. ✅

## Red flags — Anchorings to reject

| Proposed anchoring | Problem | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| "Elegance and modernity" | Applicable to anything. Zero specificity. | What exactly is elegant? Leather? Marble? Glass? |
| "Vivid and dynamic colors" | Palette description, not anchoring. | Where does the energy come from? Sport? Cooking? Code? |
| "Scandinavian minimalist style" | Design trend, not a real anchoring. | Which wood specifically? Light birch? Raw pine? Which object? |
| "Professional and trustworthy" | Abstract qualities. | Professional like a surgeon (steel, white light) or like a notary (paper, wax)? |
| "Nature and serenity" | Too vague. Which nature? | A winter pine forest (dark green, mist) ≠ a tropical beach (turquoise, sand) |

## Validation checklist

An anchoring is valid if:
- [ ] It contains at least one physical material or texture
- [ ] You can mentally draw / photograph it
- [ ] It does NOT work for a competitor in the same sector
- [ ] It produces non-arbitrary color choices (every color comes from the anchoring)
- [ ] It imposes constraints on form (radius, spacing, animation)
